# Stage B Lessons 5-8 Plan

Stage B begins from the accepted Stage A foundation and does not restart from abstract script work.
The learner already has:

- first real-word entry into Arabic
- first owned Arabic words: `كتاب` and `باب`
- first connection/form awareness inside real words
- first supported reading experience
- first tiny usage glimpse

The Stage B job is to turn that foundation into the learner's first small usable Arabic package.

## Lesson 5

- lesson number: `5`
- working title: `One More Real Word: قلم`
- core objective: recognize and recall `قلم` as the Arabic word for `pen`
- why this lesson exists after Stage A: Stage A already proved that whole-word entry works. Lesson 5 extends that trust with one more concrete high-utility noun so the learner feels they are moving forward into more real Arabic, not circling back into script drills.
- learner-visible outcome: the learner now has a third real Arabic word and can understand `book / door / pen` as a growing beginner set
- hidden self-learning outcome: the learner strengthens the habit of learning a new Arabic word as one unit of form, sound, meaning, and recall rather than as a loose translation label
- target knowledge scope: primary target `قلم = pen`; review contrast with `كتاب` and `باب`
- recommended V2 item types: `input`, `contrast`, `listenTap`, `comprehensionCheck`, `recallPrompt`, `speakResponse`
- completion evidence: the learner identifies `قلم` from audio and text, retrieves `قلم` from meaning after support is reduced, and says it once after guided exposure
- review seed priority: `wordReading` weak-point seeds for `قلم`, with contrast repair against `كتاب` if the learner mixes the two
- next-home expectation: if clean, move to Lesson 6; if weak, do a short `قلم` review-first step before the first expression lesson

## Lesson 6

- lesson number: `6`
- working title: `This Is... Your First Fixed Expression`
- core objective: understand and build the fixed beginner expression `هذا + noun` with known objects
- why this lesson exists after Stage A: Stage A ended with a tiny `هذا ...` glimpse. Lesson 6 turns that glimpse into the first true short expression without adding broad dialogue or early grammar overload.
- learner-visible outcome: the learner can already read and say very short Arabic such as `هذا كتاب` and `هذا قلم`
- hidden self-learning outcome: the learner sees that Arabic phrases can be learned by reusing known words inside a stable frame, which builds phrase-learning trust before broader sentence work
- target knowledge scope: fixed frame `هذا`; target expressions `هذا كتاب` and `هذا قلم`; low-stakes transfer check `هذا باب`
- recommended V2 item types: `input`, `modeling`, `contrast`, `listenTap`, `arrangeResponse`, `recallPrompt`, `speakResponse`
- completion evidence: the learner identifies one target expression from audio, arranges `هذا كتاب` or `هذا قلم` correctly, recalls one full expression without the model, and says one complete line after enough guided input
- review seed priority: `sentencePattern` seeds for `هذا + noun`, then weak noun-substitution seeds if the learner swaps known objects inside the frame
- next-home expectation: if clean, move to Lesson 7; if unstable, route to a short phrase review before audio-first expansion

## Lesson 7

- lesson number: `7`
- working title: `Hear What You Already Know`
- core objective: recognize the known Stage A/B beginner package from audio before relying on visible text
- why this lesson exists after Stage A: once the learner owns a few words and one short expression, Arabic should start sounding real. This lesson moves known content into the ear instead of adding more abstract explanation.
- learner-visible outcome: the learner can hear familiar Arabic and catch whether it is `book`, `door`, `pen`, or a tiny `this is ...` line
- hidden self-learning outcome: the learner understands that knowing Arabic includes audio memory and quick recognition, not only visual reading memory
- target knowledge scope: audio recognition of `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, `هذا كتاب`, and `هذا قلم`
- recommended V2 item types: `input`, `listenTap`, `contrast`, `comprehensionCheck`, `recallPrompt`, `speakResponse`
- completion evidence: the learner identifies familiar items from audio-only prompts, recovers the meaning or Arabic text for at least one heard item after visual support is removed, and repeats one known expression aloud
- review seed priority: audio weak-point seeds for confusable known items first, then `sentencePattern` seeds for heard expressions that fail to stick
- next-home expectation: if clean, move to Lesson 8; if weak, surface a short audio-review pass on the unstable items first

## Lesson 8

- lesson number: `8`
- working title: `Your First Usable Arabic Pack`
- core objective: stabilize a tiny usable Arabic pack built from three known nouns and one fixed expression frame
- why this lesson exists after Stage A: Stage B must end with a visible beginner milestone. Lesson 8 turns separate wins into one coherent package so the learner feels, “I can already read and say some Arabic.”
- learner-visible outcome: the learner can read, hear, recall, and say a small real Arabic pack: `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, and short `هذا ...` lines
- hidden self-learning outcome: the learner experiences mixed retrieval and review as the normal way to keep Arabic growing, so progress feels like a reusable package rather than four disconnected lesson wins
- target knowledge scope: mastery pack `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, `هذا كتاب`, and `هذا قلم`; low-stakes transfer check `هذا باب`
- recommended V2 item types: `input`, `contrast`, `listenTap`, `comprehensionCheck`, `arrangeResponse`, `recallPrompt`, `speakResponse`
- completion evidence: the learner succeeds in mixed reading/listening recognition, recalls at least one word and one full expression without the model, and produces one short spoken output using the known pack
- review seed priority: mixed weak-point seeds for unstable words and expressions, with `sentencePattern` and audio confusions prioritized ahead of already-stable single-word items
- next-home expectation: if stable, unlock the Stage C opener; if weak, route to a short mixed Stage B pack review before moving forward
