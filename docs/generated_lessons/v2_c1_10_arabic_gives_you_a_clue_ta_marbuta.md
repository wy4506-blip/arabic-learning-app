# 1. Working title

Arabic Gives You a Clue: `ـة`

# 2. Core objective

Help the learner notice that `ـة` can act as a helpful clue on the page in a very small controlled set of Arabic words.

# 3. Why this lesson exists after Lesson 9

Lesson 9 showed the learner that one new Arabic word, `بيت`, can be learned in a steadier way than pure translation memorization. That opens the door to the next beginner win: sometimes Arabic itself gives a small clue on the page.

Lesson 10 exists to make Arabic feel a little less random without turning into a rule lesson. The learner should not feel that they are entering grammar class. They should feel that they have discovered one small useful signal that helps them notice something about a word before they fully know it.

This lesson keeps visible learner value strong by staying inside real words and by making the clue feel practical: "I can spot this ending, and it tells me something helpful."

# 4. Learner-visible outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should feel:

- "Arabic sometimes gives me a clue on the page."
- "I can spot `ـة` in a word like `سيارة`."
- "I can see the same clue again in `كلمة`."
- "I do not have to treat every Arabic word as completely random."

# 5. Hidden self-learning outcome

The lesson quietly teaches that visible word endings can help with:

- noticing word shape more carefully
- grouping related-looking words in memory
- carrying one light category hint without formal grammar overload

The underlying beginner habit is: do not only ask "What does this word mean?" Also notice whether the word is offering a visible clue.

Here, the clue is presented in a tightly controlled beginner-safe way: `ـة` often marks a feminine noun in this lesson's tiny word set.

# 6. Target knowledge scope

Primary clue target:

- `ـة`
- beginner meaning of the clue: a helpful ending that often marks a feminine noun in this tiny controlled set

Primary clue-carrier example:

- `سيارة`
- meaning support: `car`
- learner role: main example word that makes the clue feel real

Secondary support example:

- `كلمة`
- meaning support: `word`
- learner role: lighter confirmation example, not an equally heavy vocabulary target

Known contrast anchors:

- `كتاب`
- `باب`
- `قلم`
- `بيت`

Scope boundary:

- The lesson teaches one core objective only: notice `ـة` as a helpful clue.
- This is not a full lesson on feminine nouns.
- `سيارة` carries more weight than `كلمة`; `كلمة` is mainly there to confirm the clue pattern.
- Known pack words are contrast anchors only.
- Do not introduce broader agreement rules, sentence rules, or noun-system theory.

# 7. Input design

Input should feel like clue-finding inside real Arabic words, not rule explanation.

Recommended input flow:

1. First clue-carrier word
   - Show `سيارة`
   - Give meaning support: `car`
   - Give audio
   - Lightly draw attention to the ending `ـة`
   - Learner-facing line: "This word gives you a small clue on the page."

2. Clue confirmation word
   - Show `كلمة`
   - Give meaning support: `word`
   - Give audio
   - Show that it ends the same way
   - Learner-facing line: "Here is the same clue again."

3. Contrast with known words
   - Briefly compare `سيارة` and `كلمة` against known pack words such as `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, and `بيت`
   - The point is not memorizing a list; the point is noticing that some words show a visible clue and some do not

4. Keep the phrasing light
   - Use product-like wording such as:
     - "Arabic sometimes helps you notice something early."
     - "This ending is one of those helpful clues."

Input should avoid:

- grammar terminology as the main frame
- presenting `سيارة` and `كلمة` as two equal new vocabulary goals
- broad statements about all feminine nouns in Arabic
- long explanation blocks

# 8. Recognition design

Recognition should confirm that the learner can spot the clue in real words and distinguish clue-words from non-clue words in a tiny set.

Recommended recognition sequence:

1. Word -> clue spotting
   - Show `سيارة`
   - Ask the learner to identify the helpful ending

2. Second example recognition
   - Show `كلمة`
   - Ask which part gives the same clue as in `سيارة`

3. Clue-versus-no-clue contrast
   - Mix `سيارة`, `كلمة`, and one or two known words such as `كتاب` or `بيت`
   - Ask which word shows the clue

4. Light meaning support recognition
   - Ask the learner to identify `سيارة` as `car` or `كلمة` as `word` in one or two low-load checks
   - This keeps visible Arabic value present without turning the lesson into twin vocab acquisition

Recognition items should:

- keep option sets small
- make `سيارة` the main lexical anchor
- keep `كلمة` clearly lighter-weight than a full standalone target

# 9. Recall design

Recall must produce real evidence that the learner can bring back the clue, not only see it while highlighted.

Required recall-bearing moment:

1. Clue retrieval in context
   - Show a partially hidden clue-carrier form such as `سيار_`
   - The learner must retrieve the ending `ة` as the clue-bearing completion
   - This is the main recall-bearing step

Recommended delayed recall:

2. Shared-clue recovery after interference
   - After one or two contrast items with `كتاب`, `باب`, or `بيت`, ask which other example word in this lesson shares the same clue as `سيارة`
   - Expected answer: `كلمة`
   - This should feel like recovering the clue connection, not memorizing a rule label

Optional light cue recall:

3. Clue meaning check
   - Ask what kind of helpful signal `ـة` gave in this lesson
   - Learner-facing answer style: `a clue for a feminine noun`
   - This is supportive and low-stakes, not the main mastery evidence

Recall must not be satisfied by:

- only spotting highlighted endings
- only choosing from fully guided examples with the answer already obvious
- recalling a grammar label without recovering the clue in an actual word

# 10. Output design

Output should make the learner feel that they can now actively spot a clue in Arabic.

Recommended output:

- one guided action where the learner taps or highlights the `ـة` ending inside a clue-word

Preferred output target:

- `سيارة`

Optional alternate target if the runtime supports it cleanly:

- `كلمة`

Purpose of output:

- turn clue recognition into one simple active success moment
- keep the lesson learner-friendly without forcing full grammar explanation or heavy production
- land the feeling: "I can actually notice this clue myself."

Output boundary:

- do not require the learner to explain grammatical theory
- do not require full free production of either example word
- do not make `كلمة` carry equal output weight with `سيارة`

# 11. Completion design

Completion should feel like a first pattern-awareness win, not a rule exam.

The learner should complete the lesson only after demonstrating:

- they can spot `ـة` in the main clue-carrier word `سيارة`
- they can recognize the same clue in the lighter support word `كلمة`
- they can distinguish clue-words from non-clue words in a tiny controlled set
- they can complete at least one real recall-bearing clue recovery step
- they can perform one active clue-marking output action

Completion message should make the gain visible:

- the learner can now spot one helpful Arabic clue on the page
- Arabic is starting to feel more readable and less random
- the learner is ready for the next lesson, where another small clue helps show one versus more than one

Completion should not claim:

- mastery of feminine nouns
- broad grammar knowledge
- full vocabulary ownership of both example words
- generalization to all Arabic words

# 12. Completion contract

## mastery_status

`mastered` only if the learner succeeds on clue recognition in the tiny target set and also completes at least one recall-bearing recovery of the `ـة` clue inside a word context.

`needs_review` if the learner can follow highlighted examples but cannot recover the clue when support is reduced, or if they consistently lose the contrast between clue-words and known non-clue words.

## learning_evidence

Minimum acceptable evidence:

- spotted `ـة` inside `سيارة`
- recognized that `كلمة` carries the same clue
- distinguished a clue-word from a non-clue word in a small controlled set
- completed one recall-bearing clue recovery step such as restoring `سيار_` with `ة`
- completed one guided output action highlighting or tapping the clue ending

Stronger evidence:

- handles both `سيارة` and `كلمة` cleanly without heavy prompting
- stays stable when contrasted against known words such as `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, and `بيت`
- correctly carries the learner-facing meaning of the clue as a feminine-noun hint in this lesson's tiny set

## review_seed_candidates

Priority candidates:

- `clue_spot_ta_marbuta`
- `clue_contrast_ta_marbuta_vs_no_clue`
- `clue_word_sayyara_primary`

Lower-priority support candidates:

- `clue_word_kalima_support`
- `clue_meaning_feminine_hint`

Fallback candidate if clue recovery is weak:

- `restore_ta_marbuta_in_context`

## next_home_action

If `mastered`:

- continue to Lesson 11, where the learner notices another helpful clue showing one versus more than one

If `needs_review`:

- schedule a short clue-review step before Lesson 11 so the `ـة` pattern stays available as a live reading aid

# 13. Review seed logic

Seed 1: `clue_spot_ta_marbuta`

- Trigger when the learner misses `ـة` even in the main clue-carrier word `سيارة`
- Purpose: stabilize the visual clue itself before anything broader is attempted

Seed 2: `clue_contrast_ta_marbuta_vs_no_clue`

- Trigger when the learner confuses clue-words with known non-clue words such as `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, or `بيت`
- Purpose: make the clue feel usable on the page by strengthening the contrast between clue and no-clue words

Seed 3: `clue_word_sayyara_primary`

- Trigger when the learner cannot keep the main example word stable enough to support the clue experience
- Purpose: protect the lesson's main lexical anchor without turning it into a full standalone noun lesson

Seed 4: `clue_word_kalima_support`

- Trigger when the learner loses the second example completely
- Purpose: preserve the sense that the clue appears in more than one real word, while keeping this example lighter-weight

Seed 5: `clue_meaning_feminine_hint`

- Trigger when the learner loses the beginner meaning of the clue entirely
- Purpose: restore the clue's usefulness without escalating into grammar theory

Seed priority rule:

- prioritize clue recovery weakness first
- then clue-versus-no-clue contrast weakness
- then main example instability
- keep the second example and the feminine-hint meaning as lighter support

Review boundary:

- review should still feel like re-contact with one helpful clue in real words
- do not let review expand into a general lesson on feminine noun rules

# 14. Paid-boundary note

This free lesson should feel substantial because it gives the learner the first satisfying experience that Arabic can offer a visible clue on the page.

Paid expansion value remains intact because later content can still deepen:

- many more clue-bearing patterns
- broader noun families and endings
- wider reading practice with pattern support
- richer explanations of category behavior and agreement
- broader morphology and grammar coverage when the learner is ready

Free value here is: "I can spot one helpful clue in Arabic and it makes the language feel less random."
Paid value later is: "I can grow that into a wider ability to read patterns, endings, and structure across much more Arabic."

# 15. Implementation notes

- Keep exactly one formal lesson objective: notice `ـة` as a helpful clue in a tiny controlled word set.
- Make `سيارة` the primary clue-carrier example.
- Keep `كلمة` as a lighter confirmation example, not an equally heavy second vocabulary target.
- Use known Stage B words such as `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, and `بيت` as contrast anchors only.
- Preferred V2 flow:
  - Input
  - Recognition
  - Recognition
  - Recall
  - Output
  - Completion
- Ensure there is at least one real recall-bearing step recovering the clue in context, not just spotting a highlighted ending.
- Keep option sets at 2-3 choices where possible.
- Use product-like wording such as `helpful clue`, `page clue`, or `signal`, and avoid rule-heavy copy.
- Do not require independent full-word recall for both `سيارة` and `كلمة` as if they were equal new vocabulary lessons.
- If one low-load meaning check is included for each example word, keep `سيارة` as the more salient anchor.
- Completion UI should visibly mark the learner gain as clue-finding: Arabic gave them one useful page-level hint.
