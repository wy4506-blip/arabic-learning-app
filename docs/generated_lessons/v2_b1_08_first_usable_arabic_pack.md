# 1. Working title

Your First Usable Arabic Pack

# 2. Core objective

Help the learner stabilize and use one tiny owned Arabic pack across reading, listening, recall, and short output.

# 3. Why this lesson exists after Lesson 7

By the end of Lesson 7, the learner has not only learned a few beginner items, but has started hearing them more directly from audio. That means Stage B now has enough real material to become a small usable package rather than a chain of separate wins.

Lesson 8 exists to turn Lessons 5-7 into a coherent beginner bundle. It should not feel like a bland review mix or a pile of recap questions. Its job is to make the learner feel: "These pieces now belong together. I can already do something small but real in Arabic."

This lesson also acts as the end-of-stage milestone for Stage B. The learner should leave with a visible sense that Arabic is no longer only an entry experience. It is now a small working pack they can read, hear, recall, and say.

# 4. Learner-visible outcome

By the end of the lesson, the learner should feel:

- "I now own a small Arabic pack, not just separate lesson fragments."
- "I can handle `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, and tiny `هذا ...` lines across more than one kind of task."
- "I can read some Arabic, hear some Arabic, remember it, and say a little of it."
- "I have reached a real beginner milestone."

# 5. Hidden self-learning outcome

The lesson quietly teaches that progress in Arabic becomes stronger when known items are mixed and reused across channels rather than studied one by one forever.

It reinforces that a beginner pack becomes stable through:

- mixed recognition
- mixed recall
- movement between word-level and line-level knowledge
- one small usable output step

It also prepares the learner for Stage C by showing that Arabic content can be handled as a connected system rather than as isolated cards.

# 6. Target knowledge scope

Core pack targets:

- `كتاب`
- `باب`
- `قلم`
- `هذا كتاب`
- `هذا قلم`

Optional low-stakes transfer item:

- `هذا باب`

Scope boundary:

- No new vocabulary targets.
- No new expression frame.
- No broad listening or conversation expansion.
- The objective is pack stabilization and usable handling, not adding more content.

# 7. Input design

Input should frame the lesson as a chapter-level payoff, not as another isolated drill set.

Recommended input flow:

1. Pack reveal
   - Re-show the learner's current Arabic pack as one small set:
     - `كتاب`
     - `باب`
     - `قلم`
     - `هذا كتاب`
     - `هذا قلم`
   - Learner-facing line: "These are now your first small Arabic pack."

2. Capability framing
   - Briefly show that this pack can now be handled in four ways:
     - see it
     - hear it
     - bring it back
     - say it
   - This helps the learner understand why the lesson is not "just review"

3. Stage B milestone framing
   - A short learner-facing line can signal:
     "You are no longer only entering Arabic. You can already use a small amount of it."

Input should avoid:

- introducing new explanations about grammar
- long review lectures
- adding new words to make the lesson feel bigger
- turning the lesson into a checklist of separate mini-lessons

# 8. Recognition design

Recognition should show that the learner can handle the small pack across mixed reading and listening cues.

Recommended recognition sequence:

1. Mixed reading recognition
   - Show `كتاب`, `باب`, or `قلم`
   - Ask for the correct meaning from a very small set

2. Mixed audio recognition
   - Play one known word from the pack
   - Ask the learner to identify the matching meaning or Arabic form

3. Expression recognition
   - Show or play `هذا كتاب` or `هذا قلم`
   - Ask the learner to identify the matching meaning or line

4. Word-versus-line recognition
   - Contrast a word with a line that contains it, such as `قلم` versus `هذا قلم`
   - Ask which item was seen or heard
   - This confirms that the learner is handling the pack at more than one level

Recognition items should:

- stay inside the known pack
- use small option sets
- feel varied enough to show real ownership, not random recap

# 9. Recall design

Recall must prove that the learner can bring back both a known word and a known short line from mixed cues.

Required recall-bearing steps:

1. Meaning -> word retrieval
   - Prompt with `pen`, `book`, or `door`
   - Learner must retrieve one known word from the pack
   - This can use a constrained build or other supported recall format

2. Meaning or sound -> line retrieval
   - Prompt with `this is a book` or play `هذا قلم`
   - Learner must rebuild or arrange one full known line
   - `arrangeResponse` is recommended for the line-level recall step

Recommended pack-level retrieval feel:

3. Delayed mixed recall
   - After one or two recognition items, ask the learner to recover a second known item from a different cue channel
   - Example:
     - after audio recognition, retrieve `قلم` from meaning
     - after reading recognition, arrange `هذا كتاب` from sound or meaning

Recall must not be satisfied by:

- recognition-only tapping
- repeating answers while the full target stays visible
- retrieving only words or only lines, but not both levels

# 10. Output design

Output should give the learner a visible feeling of having a usable Arabic mini-pack.

Recommended output:

- one guided `speakResponse` for a full known line, preferably `هذا كتاب` or `هذا قلم`

Optional low-stakes stretch output if the learner is stable:

- one guided output for `هذا باب`

Purpose of output:

- make the pack feel usable rather than only recognizable
- show clear progress from single-word output to short-line output
- land the end-of-stage milestone with one simple real Arabic success moment

Output boundary:

- do not require free production of new content
- do not turn output into pronunciation grading
- keep it to one short successful line, with optional low-stakes transfer only if needed

# 11. Completion design

Completion should feel like a clear Stage B graduation moment.

The learner should complete the lesson only after demonstrating:

- they can recognize items from the pack through reading and listening
- they can retrieve at least one known word from meaning or sound
- they can retrieve at least one known line from meaning or sound
- they can produce one short spoken line from the pack

Completion message should make the milestone visible:

- the learner now owns a first usable Arabic pack
- they can read, hear, recall, and say some real beginner Arabic
- Stage B is complete and the learner is ready to move into Stage C with real content already in hand

Completion should not claim:

- broad beginner fluency
- broad sentence flexibility
- broad listening ability
- mastery of content beyond the small pack

# 12. Completion contract

## mastery_status

`mastered` only if the learner succeeds on mixed pack handling across reading/listening recognition and also completes at least one recall-bearing word retrieval plus one recall-bearing line retrieval.

`needs_review` if the learner can still pass isolated items but the pack breaks down under mixed retrieval, audio contrast, or line-level recovery.

## learning_evidence

Minimum acceptable evidence:

- correctly recognized at least one known word from print
- correctly recognized at least one known item from audio
- retrieved one known word such as `قلم`, `كتاب`, or `باب` from a meaning cue
- rebuilt or arranged one full known line such as `هذا كتاب` or `هذا قلم`
- completed one guided spoken output for a known line

Stronger evidence:

- succeeds across both word-level and line-level items without heavy support
- handles a contrast such as `قلم` versus `هذا قلم`
- completes an optional low-stakes transfer check such as `هذا باب`
- shows stable performance across more than one cue channel in the same lesson

## review_seed_candidates

Priority candidates:

- `stage_b_pack_mixed_word_stability`
- `stage_b_pack_audio_mix`
- `stage_b_pack_line_recall`
- `stage_b_pack_word_vs_line_contrast`

Fallback candidate if line assembly is weak:

- `stage_b_pack_arrange_support`

Optional transfer seed if needed:

- `stage_b_pack_hadha_bab_transfer`

## next_home_action

If `mastered`:

- unlock the Stage C opener and present the result as progression from a stable beginner pack into deeper word-study and pattern awareness

If `needs_review`:

- route to a short mixed Stage B pack review before Stage C so weak items are stabilized first

# 13. Review seed logic

Seed 1: `stage_b_pack_mixed_word_stability`

- Trigger when the learner confuses `كتاب`, `باب`, and `قلم` under mixed cues
- Purpose: stabilize the core three-word pack as a usable set rather than isolated items

Seed 2: `stage_b_pack_audio_mix`

- Trigger when audio recognition is weaker than reading recognition across known items
- Purpose: keep Stage B gains balanced across ear and eye before moving on

Seed 3: `stage_b_pack_line_recall`

- Trigger when the learner knows words individually but cannot rebuild `هذا كتاب` or `هذا قلم` reliably
- Purpose: protect the pack's usable short-line layer

Seed 4: `stage_b_pack_word_vs_line_contrast`

- Trigger when the learner hears or sees the noun correctly but loses whether it appeared alone or inside a known line
- Purpose: stabilize the distinction between word-level ownership and line-level ownership

Seed 5: `stage_b_pack_hadha_bab_transfer`

- Trigger only if optional `هذا باب` transfer is used and feels unstable
- Purpose: lightly test whether the fixed frame can carry one more familiar noun without making it a new content target

Seed priority rule:

- prioritize line-level recall weakness first
- then audio imbalance
- then mixed word confusions
- then word-versus-line contrast weakness
- keep optional transfer lowest priority

Review boundary:

- review should still feel like returning to one small usable pack
- do not expand into new vocabulary, new structures, or broad listening practice

# 14. Paid-boundary note

This free lesson should feel substantial because it gives the learner a genuine end-of-stage payoff: a small pack of Arabic that can already be read, heard, recalled, and said.

Paid expansion value remains intact because later content can still deepen:

- larger vocabulary packs
- more fixed expressions and scene-based language
- broader listening variety
- more flexible sentence building
- wider reading and usage range

Free value here is: "I already have a small usable Arabic pack."
Paid value later is: "I can grow that pack into broader, deeper Arabic ability."

# 15. Implementation notes

- Keep exactly one formal lesson objective: stabilize and use one tiny owned Arabic pack across reading, listening, recall, and short output.
- Keep the pack small and closed: `كتاب`, `باب`, `قلم`, `هذا كتاب`, `هذا قلم`, with optional low-stakes `هذا باب` only.
- Do not introduce any new lexical or structural target.
- Preferred V2 flow:
  - Input
  - Mixed recognition
  - Mixed recognition with audio
  - Recall at word level
  - Recall at line level
  - Output
  - Completion
- Ensure completion requires both a word-level retrieval and a line-level retrieval so the lesson cannot degrade into shallow recap.
- Use `arrangeResponse` for at least one line-level recall step.
- Keep option sets at 2-3 choices maximum.
- Mix cue channels deliberately, but keep the total item count small enough to stay beginner-safe.
- If optional `هذا باب` is used, keep it low-stakes and do not let it function as a new objective.
- Completion UI should present this as the Stage B milestone: the learner now has a first usable Arabic pack.
