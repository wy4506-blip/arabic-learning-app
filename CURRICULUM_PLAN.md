# CURRICULUM_PLAN.md

## Mission

Generate a 16-lesson V2 Arabic foundation track for absolute beginners.

This curriculum must align with the high-level pedagogical progression commonly used in authoritative beginner Arabic curricula:
1. phonetics and script entry first
2. basic pronunciation and reading rules
3. early use of simple daily expressions
4. gradual transition from phonetics to short text handling
5. explicit learner awareness of Arabic patterns
6. explicit formation of self-learning habits

Do not copy any textbook wording, lesson titles, lesson texts, dialogues, exercises, exercise sequencing, or protected expression from any published textbook.
Do not paraphrase textbook passages.
Only follow the high-level teaching progression and capability goals.

The final goal is not broad content coverage.
The final goal is that after 16 lessons, the learner can continue learning Arabic independently at a beginner level.

---

## Product goal

The free 16-lesson track must do two things at the same time:

1. Deliver visible learner value:
   - the learner feels they have truly learned Arabic content
   - the learner can read, recognize, recall, and say some basic Arabic
   - the learner can handle very short beginner materials

2. Build long-term learner capability:
   - the learner knows how to study a new Arabic word
   - the learner knows how to study a new Arabic phrase
   - the learner understands that Arabic learning is not only translation memorization
   - the learner forms review habits and pattern awareness
   - the learner is ready to continue into paid content

This curriculum must not feel too abstract or too “method-only”.
Every stage must produce visible learning gains.

---

## Commercial design rule

The free curriculum must feel substantial, but not complete.

That means:
- it must be strong enough to make the learner trust the product
- it must create real progress and confidence
- it must create desire for further expansion
- it must not exhaust the full paid value

The free track should leave the learner feeling:
“I have truly entered Arabic, and this system works. I want more.”

---

## Curriculum architecture

The curriculum must contain 16 lessons organized into 4 stages of 4 lessons each.

### Stage A: phonetics and script entry (Lessons 1-4)
Purpose:
- reduce fear of Arabic
- build script familiarity
- establish basic pronunciation awareness
- introduce diacritics as beginner support
- introduce the app’s learning logic

### Stage B: from phonetics to minimal usable language (Lessons 5-8)
Purpose:
- move from letters/sounds into words and very short fixed expressions
- give the learner visible usable outcomes
- create the first feeling of “I can already say/read something”

### Stage C: pattern awareness and very short text handling (Lessons 9-12)
Purpose:
- introduce minimal awareness of Arabic structure
- teach how to study a word beyond translation
- introduce light awareness of gender/plural or similar foundational distinctions
- help the learner process very short beginner materials

### Stage D: self-learning ability and transfer (Lessons 13-16)
Purpose:
- teach the learner how to continue learning independently
- teach how to study a new word
- teach how to study a new phrase
- teach how to use review properly
- end with a small independent learning project

---

## Mandatory learner outcomes after 16 lessons

By the end of Lesson 16, the learner should be able to:

1. recognize the right-to-left nature of Arabic and basic script behavior
2. handle beginner diacritized forms with support
3. recognize and recall a small but meaningful set of core words and expressions
4. understand that Arabic words should be learned through form, sound, meaning, and pattern
5. show first awareness of foundational Arabic distinctions such as pattern, gender, plural, or sentence type
6. process very short beginner-level materials with guidance
7. use a repeatable process to study a new Arabic word
8. use a repeatable process to study a new Arabic phrase
9. understand the role of review in retention
10. feel ready to continue learning in paid content

## Curriculum design rules

### Rule 1: visible value in every 4-lesson block
Each stage must end with a visible learner milestone.

The learner must clearly feel:
- Stage A: “Arabic is not as scary as I thought.”
- Stage B: “I can already read/say some Arabic.”
- Stage C: “I can see that Arabic has patterns and I can start handling them.”
- Stage D: “I now know how to keep learning.”

### Rule 2: methods must be hidden inside real content learning
Do not design the free curriculum as pure study-skills lessons.
Every study-skill lesson must also teach real Arabic content.

### Rule 3: one lesson, one core objective
Every lesson must have exactly one core objective.

### Rule 4: low cognitive load
Keep each lesson manageable for an absolute beginner.

### Rule 5: V2 lesson structure
Every lesson must follow:
Input -> Recognition -> Recall -> Output -> Completion

### Rule 6: real learning evidence
Every lesson that claims learning must include recall-bearing evidence.

### Rule 7: progression trust
Every lesson must produce clear completion signals, review seed logic, and next home action.

## Required stage-by-stage lesson intentions

### Stage A intentions
Lesson 1 must introduce Arabic learning expectations and the app’s learning logic.
Lesson 2 must provide first success with a small set of core script/sound elements.
Lesson 3 must introduce basic script connection/form awareness.
Lesson 4 must introduce beginner use of diacritics and a minimal reading support concept.

### Stage B intentions
Lesson 5 must create the first true “I learned a word” experience.
Lesson 6 must create the first true “I learned a short expression” experience.
Lesson 7 must begin audio-based recognition.
Lesson 8 must consolidate the first usable beginner package.

### Stage C intentions
Lesson 9 must teach how to study an Arabic word beyond translation.
Lesson 10 must introduce one foundational Arabic distinction such as gender awareness.
Lesson 11 must introduce one foundational Arabic distinction such as plural awareness.
Lesson 12 must introduce a very short text or short structured material.

### Stage D intentions
Lesson 13 must teach how to study a new word independently.
Lesson 14 must teach how to study a new phrase independently.
Lesson 15 must teach how review stabilizes memory and learning.
Lesson 16 must act as a graduation mini-project showing self-learning transfer.

---

## Free-vs-paid design boundaries

The free 16 lessons should include:
- phonetic entry
- small but meaningful usable content
- first pattern awareness
- first self-learning method
- first short-text handling

The free 16 lessons should not fully include:
- large-scale vocabulary expansion
- broad scene coverage
- systematic grammar coverage
- extensive conversation training
- large reading corpus
- deep listening library
- advanced spaced review personalization
- broad morphology coverage

The free track should prove that the method works.
The paid track should scale the method into breadth, depth, and continuity.
